Biography:

Xavi Hernández is a retired Spanish professional footballer and current football manager, renowned for his exceptional vision, passing, and control in midfield. Often cited as one of the best midfielders in football history, Xavi's career was defined by his remarkable ability to dictate the tempo of a game and his role in the development of "tiki-taka" football. Xavi began his professional career with FC Barcelona, where he spent the majority of his playing career. At Barcelona, he won numerous titles, including eight La Liga championships, seven Copa del Rey titles, and four UEFA Champions League trophies. His partnership with Andrés Iniesta in the midfield was pivotal to Barcelona’s success and the team’s dominant style of play during the late 2000s and early 2010s. In 2015, Xavi moved to Al-Sadd SC in Qatar, where he continued to play until his retirement in 2019. His leadership and experience were invaluable, and he helped the team win domestic titles in Qatar. On the international stage, Xavi earned over 130 caps for Spain, playing a crucial role in their triumphs in the 2008 UEFA European Championship, the 2010 FIFA World Cup, and the 2012 UEFA European Championship. His performances were instrumental in Spain's golden era of international football. After retiring from playing, Xavi transitioned into coaching. He became the head coach of FC Barcelona in November 2021, bringing his deep understanding of the game and his experience as a player to his managerial role. Xavi's approach to coaching reflects his playing style, emphasizing possession-based football and technical excellence. Xavi Hernández’s legacy is marked by his contributions to Barcelona’s and Spain’s success, his influence on modern football tactics, and his role as a footballing icon both on and off the field.
