Biography:

David Beckham is a retired English professional footballer, widely known for his extraordinary passing, vision, and trademark free-kick abilities. Beckham's career spanned more than two decades, during which he played for several top European clubs including Manchester United, Real Madrid, AC Milan, and Paris Saint-Germain, as well as Major League Soccer's LA Galaxy. His influence extended beyond the football field, making him one of the most marketable athletes in the world. Beckham rose to fame at Manchester United, where he was part of the iconic "Class of '92" that dominated English football in the late 1990s and early 2000s. He helped United win six Premier League titles, two FA Cups, and the UEFA Champions League in 1999, as part of their historic treble. Beckham’s precise crossing and free-kicks were unmatched, earning him a reputation as one of the best set-piece specialists. In 2003, Beckham moved to Real Madrid, where he played a key role in the club’s "Galácticos" era, winning La Liga in 2007. His move to the LA Galaxy in 2007 helped raise the profile of Major League Soccer in the United States. Beckham also had short stints at AC Milan and Paris Saint-Germain, adding more silverware to his illustrious career. On the international stage, Beckham earned 115 caps for England, serving as the team’s captain from 2000 to 2006. He played in three FIFA World Cups and two UEFA European Championships, becoming one of the most recognizable faces in global football. Following his retirement in 2013, Beckham has remained involved in football as the co-owner of Inter Miami CF, a Major League Soccer team. He is also a global ambassador for various charitable causes and brands, continuing to maintain his influence both in and outside of football. Beckham’s legacy as a footballing icon is firmly established, with his impact transcending the sport.
