Biography:

Pelé, born Edson Arantes do Nascimento, is a Brazilian football legend celebrated for his extraordinary skill, goal-scoring ability, and impact on the sport. Often referred to as "O Rei" (The King), Pelé's career was marked by incredible achievements and a style of play that captivated audiences around the world. Pelé began his professional career with Santos FC, where he played from 1956 to 1974. During his time at Santos, he won numerous domestic and international titles, including multiple Brasileirão championships and the Copa Libertadores. His remarkable performances earned him the status of a global superstar. In 1975, Pelé joined the New York Cosmos, helping to popularize soccer in the United States and leaving a lasting legacy in the sport’s growth there. His final professional match was in 1977, marking the end of a historic career. On the international stage, Pelé is renowned for his contributions to Brazil's success in three FIFA World Cups (1958, 1962, and 1970). He remains the only player to have won the World Cup three times. His performances in these tournaments, particularly as a young player in 1958 and as a key figure in 1970, cemented his place as one of the sport's all-time greats. Pelé's impact on football extended beyond his playing career. He became a global ambassador for the sport, involved in numerous charitable activities, and remained an influential figure in football and popular culture. His legacy is marked by his contributions to the game, his iconic status, and his role in inspiring future generations of footballers.
