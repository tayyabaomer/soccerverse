Biography:

Cristiano Ronaldo is a Portuguese professional footballer known for his incredible goal-scoring ability, athleticism, and versatility. Widely regarded as one of the greatest players in the history of football, Ronaldo has had a remarkable career across several top European clubs and on the international stage. Ronaldo began his professional career with Sporting CP but gained prominence after joining Manchester United in 2003. At Manchester United, he won three Premier League titles, a UEFA Champions League title, and several domestic cups. His move to Real Madrid in 2009 marked a new chapter in his career, where he became the club's all-time top scorer. During his time at Madrid, he won four UEFA Champions League titles and numerous domestic honors, including two La Liga titles. In 2018, Ronaldo transferred to Juventus, where he continued to add to his trophy collection with two Serie A titles. In 2021, he returned to Manchester United before moving to Al-Nassr FC in Saudi Arabia in 2023. Internationally, Ronaldo has been a key player for Portugal, leading them to victory in the 2016 UEFA European Championship and the 2019 UEFA Nations League.
