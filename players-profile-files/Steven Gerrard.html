Biography:

Steven Gerrard is a retired English footballer best known for his time as captain of *Liverpool Football Club* and his leadership on and off the field. A dynamic and complete midfielder, Gerrard combined passing, tackling, leadership, and an eye for goal, making him a key figure in Liverpool's successes in the 2000s. Gerrard is most famous for his performances in big matches, including leading Liverpool to an iconic victory in the *2005 UEFA Champions League Final, where he inspired a comeback from 3-0 down against AC Milan to win on penalties, a night forever known as the “Miracle of Istanbul.” Gerrard spent 17 seasons with Liverpool, scoring **186 goals* and lifting several trophies, including two *FA Cups, three **League Cups, and one **UEFA Champions League*. Steven Gerrard was born in Whiston, England, and joined the Liverpool youth academy at the age of nine. He made his first-team debut in 1998 and went on to become one of the most iconic players in the club’s history. Known for his loyalty, Gerrard stayed with Liverpool throughout his prime years, despite interest from some of the biggest clubs in the world. Internationally, Gerrard represented *England* in three *World Cups* and two *European Championships, serving as the national team captain for several years. After finishing his playing career with **LA Galaxy* in Major League Soccer, Gerrard transitioned into management, where he led *Rangers* to their first *Scottish Premiership* title in a decade in 2021. He later managed *Aston Villa* in the Premier League.
